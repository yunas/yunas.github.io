The Best Resources for Mobile App Developers
Sloane TrugmanAuthor
09/16/2014

When creating an app, sometimes you need a little help. Crafting every little detail from scratch is enticing, but it can quickly turn into a daunting and grueling experience. Before diving headfirst into your project, ask yourself, what makes a good app?

At Fueled, we ask ourselves that question on a daily basis. There are many crucial elements to an app, from UI/UX design to back and front end development. To help you build your dream app, we’re compiled a list of the best tools and services that will help speed up the process.

iOS Fonts

Fonts are an important part of any app; they have to be legible and flow with the design. iOS Fonts is there for those who plan on creating iPhone apps. While creating your own font is tempting, there are many fonts floating around on the internet, a good portion of which are free. Just picking the best font for your app, rather than creating one, will expedite the process of creating it.

Sip

Who could forget color? Unless you’re building something monochrome, color is an integral part of crafting an app. If it’s not aesthetically pleasing, it’s doubtful that anyone will choose to download and use it. Sip lets developers sample any color from anywhere and so much more. It works with just about any programming language and the list is always growing.

Runscope

API’s are another important aspect that you should keep track of after your app has launched. Catch bugs and crashes before they happen. With a few simple lines of code, Runscope will keep up with your backend coding.

Appsee

Sometimes even after all of the coding and design is finished, apps can remain buggy or may not feel as intuitive to users as it was intended. That’s where Appsee comes in. It allows you to keep track of how your users are interacting with the app in real time with just one line of code. App analytics and touch heat maps highlight which elements are the most used and which ones can be improved.

Applause

All apps need to be tested before they’re put out into the market. At Applause, there are over 100,000 professionals who will happily test your app and ensure that it’s up to par with the quality that’s expected in the industry. Thousands of people around the world are ready to test your app to make sure it’s as functional and intuitive as possible.

Appcelerator

Apps don’t generally come with templates, but now with Appcelerator you can have one if you want. Compatibility is managed by the service while you build your app around it. Less code is required because it isn’t being built from the ground up.

Nothing screams ‘download me!’ more than a beautifully designed app. These six resources will help you get on your way to building a functional app for an MVP or longterm project. Just don’t forget to keep up with with your users, bugs, and all of the promotional elements in between!
