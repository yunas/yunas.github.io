Android App Development vs. iPhone App Development

Even though the iPhone has a smaller global market share, revenues from the iPhone’s App Store is far higher than on the Google Play Store (formerly known as Android Market). The iPhone App Store generates 85 to 95% of all revenue from applications. Business Insider notes that the Play Store has generated $330 million while iPhone’s App Store has generated $4.9 billion!

With this in mind, would you rather create an iPhone or Android app? The Android platform utilizes Java, which is a common programming language. While versatile, it is different than Apple, which utilizes Objective-C language. Anyone familiar with C and C++ will find this easy, but the App Store requires products to be approved before publication.

The differences are vast, but the bottom line is the same. If both platforms will cost the same, doesn’t it make sense to utilize a $5 billion dollar market?
