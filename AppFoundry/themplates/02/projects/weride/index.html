WeRide enlisted Fueled to create the ultimate skateboarding app. This geo-social mashup empowers users to crowdsource the greatest skating guide ever compiled. Find the best places to grind, ollie, and kick flip anywhere in the world.

Branding

Skateboarding has a strong design heritage and we knew from the outset that nailing the branding would be a critical component in the app's success. After discussing the brand with the WeRide team, we explored options around wheels and skate decks.

Diagonal

Final Brand

Ultimately the WeRide team selected the 'cutout' logo, which represents the underside of a skateboard. If you look closely you'll notice we even included the drill holes that would accommodate trunks.

User Experience

WeRide is an incredibly useful, exciting tool. While the backend is robust, the frontend navigation is dead simple. Users need to login and then browse or share skate spots.

Userflow

WeRide utilizes social login to leverage existing social graphs within the app. At a high level, once users are logged in, they are able to browse for skate spots or submit new ones. Deeper functionality allows users to tweak settings, receive push notifications about nearby skate spots, and give feedback on various types of content.

Move around to see the rest of the userflow

Wireframing the User Interface

WeRide's primary UI objective was to let users easily browse the best skate spots around them or upload new ones that the community hadn't discovered yet. The interface is all about maps, location, pin-dropping, and search.

As is typical at Fueled, a bit of our illustration prowess crept into the UI process. You can see that coming through in the way many UI elements that might be left as placeholder icons by lesser designers were in this instance sharply stylized.

Bringing it together

With much of the heavy lifting out of the way during UI, we focused on polishing off design elements, including detailed navigational iconography and adding depth to individual screen elements.

For example, you'll notice we used a skate wheel as a pie chart to indicate a venue's rating from the community.

Flat Menus

A bit ahead of the curve with flat design, we designed a clean interface to indicate the amenities available at a particular skate spot.

We created a set of custom icons to highlight things like rails, stairs, ledges, and gaps. A custom action-confirmation overlay continues the cohesive hexagonal design language, referential of the screws used to hold skateboard trucks onto the deck.

Find The Best Skate Spots Around You

WeRide hit the mark right on the head with the idea of creating a disruptive product that replaced hundreds of online blog posts, message boards, and print magazines with a single go-to directory of the worlds skate spots.

Ryan Matzner, Director at Fueled

The Result

WeRide is the ultimate skateboarding tool. The app is a powerful yet simple tool that will change the sport forever, shifting the information paradigm and democratizing the sport.
